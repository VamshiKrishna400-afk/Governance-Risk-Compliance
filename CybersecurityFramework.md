# What is Cybersecruity Framework?
A **cybersecurity framework** is a set of **guidelines, best practices, standards,** and **processes** that helps organizations protect 
their systems, networks, and data from cyber threats. It provides a structured approach to managing cybersecurity risks.

Cybersecurity frameworks were created to:

- Reduce cybersecurity risks.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Provide a standard approach to security.
- Help organizations prepare for, detect, respond to, and recover from cyberattacks.

Businesses use cybersecurity frameworks to:

- Protect sensitive data and systems.
- Reduce cyber risks.
- Meet legal and regulatory requirements.
- Improve security management.
- Build customer trust.
- Prepare for and recover from cyber incidents.

### Advantages
- Improves cybersecurity posture.
- Provides a standardized security approach.
- Helps identify and manage risks.
- Supports regulatory compliance.
- Reduces the impact of cyberattacks.
- Improves incident response and recovery.
- Builds customer and stakeholder confidence.

### Disadvantages
- Can be expensive to implement.
- Requires skilled cybersecurity professionals.
- Takes time to fully adopt.
- Needs continuous updates and maintenance.
- Does not guarantee complete protection against cyberattacks.
- May be complex for small organizations.
