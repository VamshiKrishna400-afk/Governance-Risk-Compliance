# General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

## What is GDPR?

The **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** is a privacy law introduced by the **European Union (EU)** to protect the personal data and privacy of individuals. It provides a common set of data protection rules across all EU member states.

- **Effective Date:** 25 May 2018
- **Applies To:** Organizations that process the personal data of EU citizens, regardless of where the organization is located.

---

# Who Does GDPR Apply To?

GDPR applies to:

- Businesses located **inside the EU**
- Businesses located **outside the EU** if they collect or process the personal data of EU citizens
- Anyone who:
  - Collects data
  - Records data
  - Stores data
  - Organizes data
  - Processes or analyzes data
  - Shares data
  - Deletes or modifies data

### Example

> An online shopping company in **India** sells products to customers in **Germany**. Since it collects the personal data of EU citizens, it must comply with GDPR.

---

# Personal Data Covered Under GDPR

Personal data includes any information that can identify an individual, such as:

- Full name
- Home address
- Email address
- Phone number
- Passport or National ID number
- IP address
- Location data
- Cookies
- Biometric information
- Financial information

### Example

> A company stores a customer's **email address** and **purchase history**. Both are considered personal data under GDPR.

---

# Right to Request Information (Right of Access)

Users have the right to request information about:

- What personal data is collected
- Why it is collected
- How it is processed
- Who has access to it
- How long it is stored

Organizations must:

- Have a clear process for handling requests
- Verify the user's identity
- Not modify or delete requested records
- Respond within **one month**
- Provide the information in a readable and portable format
- Keep records of the request and response

### Example

> A customer asks an online bank for all personal information stored about them. The bank must provide it within one month.

---

# Data Processing Activities Covered by GDPR

GDPR applies to organizations involved in:

- Collecting data
- Recording data
- Organizing data
- Storing data
- Updating data
- Retrieving data
- Processing data
- Analyzing data
- Sharing data
- Deleting data

Users have the right to know whether their data is used for:

- Data segmentation
- Personalized offers
- Targeted advertising
- Personalized content
- Marketing campaigns

### Example

> An e-commerce website groups customers based on previous purchases and sends personalized discounts. This processing is covered by GDPR.

---

# Third-Party Data Sharing

Organizations must disclose when personal data is shared with third parties, including:

- CRM (Customer Relationship Management) systems
- Cloud storage providers
- Payment gateways
- Marketing platforms
- Analytics services

### Example

> A company stores customer data in a CRM and uses Google Analytics for website analysis. Users must be informed about these third-party services.

---

# Right to Data Portability

Users have the right to receive their personal data:

- In a structured format
- In a commonly used format
- In a machine-readable format
- So it can be transferred to another service provider

Common formats include:

- CSV
- JSON
- XML

### Example

> A user switches to another music streaming platform and requests a copy of their playlists and account information.

---

# Right to be Forgotten (Right to Erasure)

Users have the right to request deletion of their personal data when:

- The data is no longer needed
- Consent has been withdrawn
- The data was processed unlawfully
- The user objects to processing without a valid legal reason

Organizations must erase the data unless they are legally required to keep it.

### Example

> A user deletes their social media account and requests that all personal information be permanently removed.

---

# Consent

Organizations must obtain **clear and explicit consent** before collecting or processing personal data.

Consent must be:

- Freely given
- Specific
- Informed
- Unambiguous
- Easy to withdraw

### Example

> A website displays a cookie consent banner that allows users to accept or reject tracking cookies.

---

# Data Breach Notification

If a personal data breach occurs, organizations must:

- Notify the appropriate data protection authority within **72 hours** (where required)
- Inform affected users if the breach poses a high risk to their rights and freedoms

### Example

> A hacker gains access to customer account information. The company must report the breach and notify affected users if required.

---

# Data Protection by Design and by Default

Organizations should protect personal data by:

- Collecting only necessary data
- Encrypting sensitive information
- Restricting access
- Using secure storage
- Implementing privacy from the beginning of system design

### Example

> A mobile application requests access to the user's location only while the app is being used instead of tracking continuously.

---

# Accountability

Organizations must demonstrate GDPR compliance by:

- Maintaining records of data processing
- Training employees
- Conducting security assessments
- Implementing privacy policies
- Keeping documentation of compliance activities

### Example

> A company maintains records showing when customer consent was obtained and how personal data is protected.

---

# Penalties for Non-Compliance

Organizations that violate GDPR may face fines of:

- Up to **€20 million**, or
- **4% of the organization's annual global turnover**, whichever is higher

### Example

> A company collects customer data without consent and suffers a preventable data breach. It may be fined under GDPR.
