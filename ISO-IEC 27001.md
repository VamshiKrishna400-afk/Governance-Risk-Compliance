# ISO/IEC 27001

The **ISO/IEC 27001** standard is an internationally recognized standard that specifies the requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an **Information Security Management System (ISMS)**. It helps organizations systematically identify, assess, treat, and manage information security risks.

- Developed jointly by the **International Organization for Standardization (ISO)** and the **International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)**.
- First published in **October 2005**.
- Revised in **2013**.
- Latest version is **ISO/IEC 27001:2022**, published in **October 2022**.
- It is a **certifiable** international standard, allowing organizations to demonstrate compliance through independent audits.

The seven mandatory implementation clauses of ISO/IEC 27001 are:

1. Context of the Organization
2. Leadership
3. Planning
4. Support
5. Operation
6. Performance Evaluation
7. Improvement

---

## Why this matters

- Protects sensitive business and customer information.
- Reduces the risk of cyberattacks and data breaches.
- Builds customer and stakeholder trust.
- Helps meet legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements.
- Demonstrates commitment to information security.
- Improves business resilience through continual improvement.

---

## 1. Context of the Organization

***Understand the organization and its security requirements***

- Identify internal and external issues affecting information security.
- Understand the needs of customers, regulators, and other interested parties.
- Define the scope of the ISMS.
- Identify information assets that require protection.

### Real world example

***Scenario:*** A healthcare company is implementing an ISMS.

- Identifies patient records, medical devices, and cloud systems as critical assets.
- Reviews legal requirements such as HIPAA and local privacy laws.
- Defines that the ISMS covers hospitals, remote offices, and cloud infrastructure.

***Result:*** The organization understands what needs protection and clearly defines the scope of its ISMS.

---

## 2. Leadership

***Establish leadership and commitment to information security***

- Create an Information Security Policy.
- Assign cybersecurity roles and responsibilities.
- Ensure top management supports the ISMS.
- Promote a culture of information security.

### Real world example

***Scenario:*** A software company adopts ISO/IEC 27001.

- The CEO approves the Information Security Policy.
- A Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) is appointed.
- Department managers become responsible for implementing security controls.

***Result:*** Information security becomes a shared responsibility across the organization.

---

## 3. Planning

***Identify, assess, and treat information security risks***

- Perform risk assessments.
- Identify threats and vulnerabilities.
- Define security objectives.
- Create risk treatment plans.
- Plan changes to the ISMS.

### Real world example

***Scenario:*** A company identifies ransomware as a major business risk.

- Performs a risk assessment.
- Determines the likelihood and impact of ransomware.
- Implements Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), endpoint protection, and regular backups.
- Documents the selected risk treatment options.

***Result:*** Risks are understood, prioritized, and managed effectively.

---

## 4. Support

***Provide the resources needed to operate the ISMS***

- Allocate resources for information security.
- Train employees on security awareness.
- Maintain security documentation.
- Ensure staff are competent to perform security responsibilities.
- Promote communication about security.

### Real world example

***Scenario:*** A company wants to improve employee security awareness.

- Conducts annual cybersecurity awareness training.
- Performs phishing simulation exercises.
- Maintains documented security policies and procedures.
- Ensures new employees complete security training during onboarding.

***Result:*** Employees understand security responsibilities and help protect organizational information.

---

## 5. Operation

***Implement and operate information security controls***

- Implement risk treatment plans.
- Perform Vulnerability Assessments (VA).
- Conduct Penetration Testing (PT).
- Manage security incidents.
- Apply access controls and encryption.
- Monitor operational security.

### Real world example

***Scenario:*** A company secures its production environment.

- Performs monthly vulnerability scans.
- Conducts annual penetration testing.
- Uses SIEM and IDS/IPS for continuous monitoring.
- Responds to incidents using its Incident Response Plan.

***Result:*** Security controls actively protect the organization's systems and data.

---

## 6. Performance Evaluation

***Measure and evaluate the effectiveness of the ISMS***

- Monitor security performance.
- Conduct internal audits.
- Review security objectives.
- Perform management reviews.
- Identify areas for improvement.

### Real world example

***Scenario:*** A company reviews its ISMS every quarter.

- Audits compliance with security policies.
- Reviews security incidents and response times.
- Evaluates whether security objectives have been achieved.
- Management reviews audit findings and approves improvements.

***Result:*** The ISMS is continuously monitored and its effectiveness is measured.

---

## 7. Improvement

***Continually improve the ISMS***

- Correct non-conformities.
- Implement corrective actions.
- Improve security controls.
- Update policies and procedures.
- Learn from incidents and audit findings.

### Real world example

***Scenario:*** An employee falls victim to a phishing attack.

- The incident is investigated.
- Email filtering rules are updated.
- Additional phishing awareness training is provided.
- The Incident Response Plan is improved based on lessons learned.

***Result:*** The organization strengthens its security posture and becomes more resilient against future attacks.

## Annex A Security Controls

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 includes **Annex A**, which provides a reference set of **93 security controls** organized into four categories.

### 1. Organizational Controls (37)

- Information Security Policies
- Risk Management
- Asset Management
- Supplier Security
- Incident Management
- Business Continuity

### 2. People Controls (8)

- Background Verification
- Security Awareness Training
- Acceptable Use
- Disciplinary Process

### 3. Physical Controls (14)

- Secure Areas
- Physical Entry Controls
- CCTV
- Equipment Protection
- Secure Disposal

### 4. Technological Controls (34)

- Access Control
- Encryption
- Firewalls
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Logging and Monitoring
- Vulnerability Management
- Malware Protection
- Backup and Recovery

---

## Certification Process

1. Define the scope of the ISMS.
2. Perform a risk assessment.
3. Implement security controls.
4. Conduct internal audits.
5. Perform a management review.
6. Complete a Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review).
7. Complete a Stage 2 Audit (Implementation Audit).
8. Receive ISO/IEC 27001 Certification.
9. Undergo annual surveillance audits.
10. Renew certification every three years.
