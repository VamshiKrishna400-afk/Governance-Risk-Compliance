# NIST-Cybersecurity-Framework
The NIST Cybersecurity framework (CSF) is a set of voluntary guidelines and best practices that helps organizations identify, assess, manage, and reduce cybersecurity risks. It is widely used by businesses and governement organizations.

- Developed by **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)**, a US governement agency.
-  Ordered by **Executive Order 13636, signed by President Barack Obama in 2013.**
- First Released in **February 12, 2014**.
- Latest Version is NIST CSF 2.0, released in February 2024.

The six core functions of NIST CSF 2.0 are:
1. Govern
2. Identify
3. Protect
4. Detect
5. Respond
6. Recover

## Why this matters
- 50% of all cyberattacks target small businesses.     
- 60% of victims close within 6 months.    
- The NIST CSF is a roadmap to redue risk and protect your business.

## 1. Govern
***Set security policies and manage cybersecurity***
- Create cybersecurity policies and security goals.
- Assign cybersecurity roles and responsibilities.
- Manage risks and ensure compliance with security standards and regulations.
### Real world example
***Scenario:*** A bank wants to improve its cybersecurity.

- Creates a cybersecurity policy for all employees.
- Assigns a Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) to oversee security.
- Requires employees to complete cybersecurity awareness training every year.
- Reviews cybersecurity risks and security reports every quarter.

***Result:*** Everyone knows the security rules, their responsibilities, and how cybersecurity is managed.

## 2. Identify 
***Know your assets and risks***
- Identify hardware, software and data.
- Know what's critical to your business.
- Understand who has access and where the risks lie.
### Real world example
***Scenario:*** A company is preparing to secure its IT infrastructure.

- Creates an inventory of all laptops, servers, mobile devices, and cloud services.
- Lists all business applications (e.g., Microsoft 365, Salesforce, ERP systems).
- Identifies sensitive data such as customer records and financial information.
- Determines which employees have access to each system and identifies outdated software that could pose security risks.

***Result:*** The company knows what it owns, what is most important, who can access it, and where potential risks exist.

## 3. Protect 
***Safeguard your critical assets***
- Strong passwords and access controls.
- Multi factor authentication.
- Keep software up to date.
- Train staff on cyber hygeine and back important data regularly.
### Real world example
***Scenario:*** A company wants to prevent cyberattacks before they happen.

- Employees use strong passwords and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to access company accounts.
- The IT team regularly updates Windows, antivirus software, and business applications to fix security vulnerabilities.
- Employees receive cybersecurity awareness training and are instructed not to plug in unknown USB drives found in the office or outside.
- The company automatically backs up important business data to a secure cloud location every day.

***Result:*** The company reduces the chances of unauthorized access, malware infections, and data loss.

## 4. Detect
***Spot incidents early***
- Monitor for suspicious activity.
- Setup alerts for unusual logins and behaviors.
- Designate someone to review logs and reports.
- Timely discovery.
### Real world example
***Scenario:*** A company wants to identify cyber threats as quickly as possible.

- The IT team continuously monitors network traffic and user activity for suspicious behavior.
- The system generates an alert when an employee's account logs in from another country at an unusual time.
- A security analyst reviews system logs and security reports every day.
- The company uses IDS/IPS and a SIEM platform to detect attacks, generate alerts, and investigate suspicious activities quickly.

***Result:*** The company detects potential cyberattacks early and can respond before significant damage occurs.

## 5. Respond
***Be ready to act***
- Have an incident response plan.
- Know who to call and what steps to take.
- Communicate clearly with staff and customers during an incident.
### Real world example
***Scenario:*** A company's employee clicks on a phishing email, and ransomware begins spreading.

- The IT team follows the company's Incident Response Plan (IRP) immediately.
- The infected computer is isolated from the network, and the cybersecurity team is notified.
- Management informs employees about the incident and communicates with affected customers about the situation and recovery efforts.

***Result:*** The attack is contained quickly, minimizing damage and reducing downtime.

## 6. Recover
***Restore and improve***
- Restore data from backups.
- Resume business operations quickly.
- Analyze what happened and improve defenses.
### Real world example
***Scenario:*** After the ransomware attack has been contained.

- The IT team restores important files and systems from secure backups.
- Critical business services, such as email and customer portals, are brought back online as quickly as possible.
- The company investigates how the attack occurred, patches the vulnerability, updates security policies, and provides additional employee training to prevent similar incidents.

***Result:*** The organization resumes normal operations and improves its cybersecurity to better withstand future attacks.
